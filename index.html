Problem Statement

Dopamine → burnout

People across ages are struggling to complete everyday responsibilities because tasks that lack immediate intrinsic reward trigger procrastination and overwhelm, reducing productivity and well-being. How can we help that problem?

Background

We focus specifically on burnout-related procrastination, not clinical burnout treatment. QuestLog does not treat burnout clinically; it supports motivation through behavioral design inspired by reward-system research.

Academic burnout functions as a chronic state of exhaustion that downregulates dopamine receptors, transforming educational challenges into a source of dread rather than achievement. This chemical imbalance leads to a profound sense of detachment and cognitive decline, causing a sharp drop in academic performance and a rise in long-term psychological distress.

Gamify?

To gamify is to apply game-design elements and principles (such as point scoring, competition, and rules of play) to non-game activities to increase engagement and motivation.

Why RPG specifically?

  • 🎮 Progression-based — XP, levels, and quests give visible, continuous progress
  • 🎯 Small actions matter — even minor tasks contribute to long-term growth
  • 🧠 Restores self-efficacy — users feel capable again by "winning" small battles
  • 🔁 Intrinsic motivation loop — effort → reward → progress → desire to continue

Gamification is already accepted and normalized (Duolingo, fitness apps), so users are familiar with RPG concepts with no learning curve. When motivation is broken, systems that rely on motivation fail — RPG mechanics rebuild motivation by turning effort into visible progress.

Related Work

Habitica, Duolingo, Fitbit & Nike Run Club have all explored gamified motivation. What's different: Habitica gamifies discipline, while QuestLog gamifies motivation — better suited for burned-out users who struggle to even start.

  • The 80% Threshold: over 80% of the "Class of 2024" experienced burnout during their degree, nearly 30% "frequently" (Higher Ed Dive / Handshake 2024).
  • Retention Crisis: one in three students considers leaving their program due to emotional stress — the leading predictor of college dropouts (Lumina Foundation / Gallup, 2024–2025).
  • The Flourishing Gap: the 2024–2025 Healthy Minds Study found "flourishing" dropped to 36%.
  • Dopamine Deficiency: chronic burnout is linked to "blunted" dopamine pathways that stall the drive to complete tasks (Cleveland Clinic 2024, MDPI 2025).

Approach

Our contribution is translating burnout and motivation research into a low-friction, game-based system that rewards effort consistently without judgment. XP is measured by time spent, making rewards objective and reducing decision fatigue and self-judgment. The system rewards effort, not difficulty or perfection.

XP = minutes × class_multiplier × energy_modifier

Burnout-safe streaks

Not a "perfect streak" — a momentum streak. Missing a day pauses progress instead of erasing it, buffs gently fade instead of being lost, and returning after an absence earns a "welcome back" bonus rather than a penalty. Social warmth beats accountability pressure.

Verification / Maintenance

DegreeOfLabor is a productivity and motivation-support tool, not a medical or therapeutic intervention. "Dopamine" and burnout research are used as design inspiration to explain user behavior, not as clinical treatment claims. Our primary focus is college and high-school students experiencing burnout-related procrastination.
